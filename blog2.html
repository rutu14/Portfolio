Bhagavad Gita

An ancient book that instructs you how to live your life. What drew me to this book? A thought that occurs to me as I write this page. The book's setting is based on a fight between relatives. The major outcome is that good triumphs over evil, but doesn't every literature and narrative on the planet state this?



So far, the best explanation I've heard is"Conquer your Fears". When Arjuna is undecided about fighting the adharma,  Krishna tells him the verses. This helps to conquer his fears.  This notion made me realise that your weaknesses/failures are only there to help you succeed if you work on them.



Leaving one's comfort zone is undoubtedly difficult. So, with a head full of ideas and plans, I want to overcome my few worries. Well, it all started with this page, which was my first step in chronicling my thoughts.
